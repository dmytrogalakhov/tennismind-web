Jannik Sinner came back from two sets down to defeat Miomir Kecmanovic in five sets on Centre Court, doing so while bleeding through his shoe from a foot injury. The world No. 1 had won just one of his past nine fifth-set matches entering the day, yet raised his level decisively when it mattered — winning all 14 points behind his first serve in the deciding set to close out the match. Kecmanovic, eyeing his first top-10 win at a major, had the crowd on his feet and Sinner on the ropes, meaning the defending champion now heads into round two with questions about his fitness and consistency on grass.
