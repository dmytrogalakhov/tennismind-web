Jannik Sinner extended his Masters 1000 winning streak to 34 matches by defeating Casper Ruud 6-4, 6-4 in the Rome final, surpassing Novak Djokovic's previous record of 31 consecutive wins set in 2011. The victory also made Sinner the second player ever to complete the Career Golden Masters (winning all nine Masters 1000 events) and ended Italy's 50-year wait for a Rome title. Sinner has now won six straight Masters 1000 titles dating back to last year's Paris Masters.
