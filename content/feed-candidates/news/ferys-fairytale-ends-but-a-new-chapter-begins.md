Arthur Fery held his own for the first 45 minutes against Zverev before a double fault and loose forehand unravelled his serve, and the French Open champion pulled away to end the wildcard's run. The Guardian noted Fery was 'the second-lowest ranked Wimbledon men's semi-finalist in the open era' — a marker that will define how the tennis world now sees him. Fery has already said he plans to speak with Emma Raducanu, whose own surprise Slam breakthrough drew immediate comparisons with his run here.
