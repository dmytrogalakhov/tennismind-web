Arthur Fery has made history at Wimbledon, becoming the first British wildcard to reach the quarterfinals in the professional era. The 23-year-old, who entered the draw unranked in the tournament's seedings, defeated Grigor Dimitrov in the fourth round to advance. Fery is now the last British player standing in either the men's or women's singles draws, keeping the home crowd's hopes alive. It marks the deepest Grand Slam run of his career.
