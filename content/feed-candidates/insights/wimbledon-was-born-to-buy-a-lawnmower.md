The first Wimbledon Championships in 1877 were not organised out of passion for tennis — they were scheduled specifically to raise money to purchase a pony-drawn roller for the All England Club's croquet lawns. Tennis had only been introduced to the club two years earlier, in 1875, as a secondary activity. The sport that now generates £64.2 million in prize money alone was originally a fundraising sideshow for a piece of groundskeeping equipment.
