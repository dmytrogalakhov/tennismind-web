Novak Djokovic just became the first man to appear in 82 Grand Slam tournaments, surpassing the previous record of 81 shared by Roger Federer and Feliciano Lopez. At 39, he's also the first player ever to compete in 120 matches at a single Grand Slam venue, eclipsing Federer's 119 matches at Wimbledon. This longevity milestone shows how the modern era has extended elite careers far beyond what was previously possible.
