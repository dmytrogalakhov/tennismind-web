The French Open's namesake, Roland Garros, was a pioneering French aviator and war hero who was more interested in football, rugby and cycling than tennis. He's best remembered for equipping fighter planes with machine guns that could fire through propellers. The stadium was built and named after him in 1928 when France needed to defend the Davis Cup, creating an ironic legacy where tennis's most prestigious clay court tournament honors someone who barely played the sport.
