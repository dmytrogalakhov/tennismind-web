The All England Club was founded as a croquet club, and lawn tennis was only introduced in 1877 to help boost the club's flagging popularity. The first championships weren't even called Wimbledon — they were officially 'The All England Croquet and Lawn Tennis Club Meeting.' The sport added as a revenue fix went on to become the most prestigious tennis tournament on earth, while the last croquet lawn was finally sacrificed for an additional tennis court as recently as 2007.
