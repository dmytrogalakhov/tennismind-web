The men's singles champion receives the Coupe des Mousquetaires, named after France's 'Four Musketeers' of tennis: Jean Borotra, Jacques Brugnon, Henri Cochet, and René Lacoste. These four French players dominated world tennis in the 1920s and 1930s, with Borotra winning Roland Garros twice himself. The trophy connects every modern champion to this golden era of French tennis excellence.
