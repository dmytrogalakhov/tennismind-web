One theory suggests tennis's 15-30-40 scoring system originated when courts were 90 yards long (45 per side) and players moved 15 feet forward with each point scored. The third point brought both players to the net, explaining why the final point was shorter at just 10 yards. While modern courts are only 78 feet, this physical movement theory explains tennis's unique scoring better than the popular 'clock face' explanation.
