The tournament's namesake, Roland Garros, was a French aviator and war hero who was more interested in football, rugby, and cycling than tennis. Despite having no connection to tennis, his name became synonymous with the sport's most prestigious clay court championship. This makes Roland Garros unique among Grand Slams — named not for a tennis pioneer, but for a pilot who died in World War I.
