In 1956, Althea Gibson became the first African-American to win the French Open, predating Serena Williams' dominance by decades. Gibson's victory came during an era when tennis was still largely segregated, making her triumph particularly significant. Her breakthrough at Roland Garros helped pave the way for future generations of diverse champions in tennis.
