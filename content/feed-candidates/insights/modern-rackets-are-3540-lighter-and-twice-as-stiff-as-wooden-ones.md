Early wooden rackets weighed 14–15 ounces and were structurally compromised because wood lacks uniform strength across both axes — manufacturers had to keep adding mass just to achieve adequate stiffness. The shift to carbon fibre and composite materials produced frames that are 35–40% lighter and twice as stiff as their wooden counterparts. That combination — less mass, more stiffness — is what unlocked the modern power baseline game, since players can swing faster without sacrificing frame stability at impact.
