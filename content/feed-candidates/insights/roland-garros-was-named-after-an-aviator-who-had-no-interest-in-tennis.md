The tournament is named after Roland Garros, a French aviator who was more interested in football, rugby and cycling than tennis. He's best remembered for equipping fighter planes with machine guns through the propeller and making the first solo flight across the Mediterranean in 1913. The stadium was built in 1928 when France needed to defend the Davis Cup, honoring the war hero despite his lack of connection to tennis.
