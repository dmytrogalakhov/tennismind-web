The science behind tennis rackets involves complex physics of string and frame vibration when the ball makes contact. Laboratory scientists now use mathematics to calculate the precise effects of weight, size, and material changes, while computer-aided design allows exact calculation of material rigidity and center of gravity. This shift from craftmanship to scientific precision explains why modern rackets can be both lighter and more powerful than ever before.
