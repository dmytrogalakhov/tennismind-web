Across professional tennis tournaments, the champion consistently receives 16-18% of the entire prize money pool, which is exactly double what the runner-up earns. This steep prize structure is based on 'tournament theory' — the idea that large differentials between rounds maximize player effort and performance. The exponential payout system means a first-round Miami Open loser gets $23,760 while the champion earns $1.12 million.
