Roland Garros became the first Grand Slam tournament to allow both amateur and professional players to compete officially in 1968, launching the Open Era. This groundbreaking decision preceded the other three Grand Slams in opening their doors to professionals. The move transformed tennis from an amateur sport into the global professional spectacle we know today.
