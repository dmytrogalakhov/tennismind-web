So he grabs a ball and drives it, hard, into nowhere. Long after the point is dead. No target, no reason — just the frustration of a man losing a set he thought he'd bought.

And then the strangest sound at Wimbledon. Boos. From _this_ crowd — the one that claps double faults politely, the one that treats the All England Club like a cathedral. They turned on their seven-time champion. All of them.

He'd already been warned once. Earlier in the match, the umpire flagged him for language. This wasn't a first offense in a bad afternoon. This was the second act.

And here's what makes it land differently than a normal tantrum. Sitting in the commentary booth was John McEnroe — a man who knows something about on-court rage — and he didn't reach for nostalgia. He reached for the record. _Got defaulted at the US Open when he did that and hit a lineswoman. Almost happened in Australia; he barely missed a ballboy._

That's not a hot take from the stands. That's a Hall of Famer, live on the BBC, reminding everyone that when Novak Djokovic hits a ball in anger, it has a history — and the history includes people getting hurt.

He won the match. Reached the quarterfinals. Passed Federer's win record on the same afternoon — 106 matches, the most by any man in the history of this tournament. The greatest to ever hold a racket, writing another line in a record book nobody may ever touch.

Which is exactly why the ball is the wrong story to be telling on the day he broke the record.

To his credit, he said it himself afterward. _The outbursts, the meltdowns, I had a few of those today. I apologise._ Good. That's the right word. But an apology is what you say after — and at 39, seven titles deep on this court, with a default and a near-miss already in the file, the question isn't whether he's sorry.

It's why a man with nothing left to prove still can't let a lost set go without firing a ball he doesn't need to fire.

You're the greatest of all time. The record is yours. The crowd was yours, until you gave them a reason not to be.

Some things you don't get to blame on the heat.
