Around 54,000 tennis balls are used across the Wimbledon fortnight, with balls inspected and swapped out every seven to nine games to maintain consistent performance on the grass surface. With 254 singles matches alone, that works out to roughly 213 balls per match on average. On grass, where ball condition directly affects skid and bounce height, the replacement cadence is tighter than on slower surfaces — making ball management one of the less visible but operationally significant parts of running the tournament.
