MEN'S DRAW
History was made on Centre Court, a grass-court giant fell to injury, and the quarterfinal picture snapped into focus.

• Djokovic🇷🇸 def. Safiullin🇷🇺 in four — a 7-6 opening set tiebreak settled, Djokovic pulled clear to record his 106th Wimbledon singles win, breaking his tie with Federer for the all-time record; the milestone arrived on the sport's most storied surface, and his section of the draw now looks his to lose
• Sinner🇮🇹 def. Mochizuki🇯🇵 in straight sets — a 7-6(0) second-set bagel tiebreak underlines the defending champion's control; he moves through without dropping a set, and the top half of the draw is consolidating around a potential Djokovic collision
• Struff🇩🇪 def. Hurkacz🇵🇱 — Hurkacz, who had been one of the tournament's most credible title threats, was forced to retire in the fifth set after clawing back from two sets down; a heartbreaking end to a genuine contender's campaign, and Struff advances to the quarterfinals as the draw's most unlikely survivor
• Auger-Aliassime🇨🇦 def. Davidovich Fokina🇪🇸 in five — Davidovich Fokina collapsed on a break point in the fourth set, recovered, then began visibly limping, in scenes that drew comparisons to football theatrics; Auger-Aliassime held his nerve through the chaos to close out the fifth 6-1, reaching the quarterfinals and continuing a grass-court run that now demands to be taken seriously

WOMEN'S DRAW
The world No. 1 is gone, the defending champion is gone, and Wimbledon's women's draw has never looked more open.

• Osaka🇯🇵 def. Sabalenka🇧🇾 — the day's defining result; Osaka crushed her serve and groundstrokes for a fourth consecutive match, pushing Sabalenka onto her heels in a 6-2, 7-6(2) demolition that is her biggest win since her last Grand Slam title in 2021; with the world No. 1 eliminated, Osaka is now a genuine quarterfinal threat in a draw that has lost its anchor
• Muchova🇨🇿 def. Krejcikova🇨🇿 in three — the defending champion is out; Muchova took the first set, dropped the second, then controlled the third to eliminate the titleholder and open the bottom half of the draw entirely
• Gauff🇺🇸 def. Bencic🇨🇭 in three — dropped the opener before winning 12 of the last 16 games; Gauff is through to the quarterfinals, and with Sabalenka and Krejcikova both gone, she is now among the draw's leading contenders
• Pegula🇺🇸 def. Jovic🇺🇸 in three — the veteran American dropped the first set to her teenage compatriot before roaring back; she advances into the quarterfinals of a draw that has shed its top names around her
