MEN'S DRAW
Sinner erased the memory of January's Australian Open defeat and ended Djokovic's pursuit of a 25th Grand Slam title in one of the most clinical semifinal performances this tournament has seen.

• Sinner🇮🇹 def. Djokovic🇷🇸 in straight sets — six months after Djokovic stunned him in a five-set Melbourne comeback, Sinner reversed the result with ruthless efficiency, winning 6-4, 6-4, 6-4 in two hours and twenty minutes; a lashing forehand in the ninth game of the first set broke the match open, and Djokovic — who admitted he was "half a step late on basically any shot" — never found a way back; at 39, his bid for a record 25th major is over, and Sinner advances to the final as the commanding favourite
• Zverev🇩🇪 def. Fery🇬🇧 in straight sets — the British wildcard who had dismantled two top-ten seeds and made history as the first British wildcard to reach a Slam semifinal in the Open Era is stopped here; Zverev moves into the final without dropping a set in either semifinal match, and Sunday's final is a meeting of the tour's two most dominant players
