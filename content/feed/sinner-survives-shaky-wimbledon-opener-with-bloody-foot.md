Jannik Sinner got through his first-round match but failed to make a dominant statement, with ESPN reporting an erratic performance that included a bloody foot. The world No. 1 and reigning Wimbledon champion showed enough vulnerability that opponents will take note heading into the second round. Sinner defends his Wimbledon title having already banked a 34-match Masters 1000 streak this season — any wobble in the draw is significant given the points he has on the line.
