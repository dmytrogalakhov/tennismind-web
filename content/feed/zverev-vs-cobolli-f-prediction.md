Zverev's experience and superior serve meet Cobolli's fearless clay-court power in a final where the German's mental baggage could finally matter. Cobolli can exploit this if he starts aggressively with his dominant forehand and forces Zverev into passive rallies, especially after beating him in Munich and getting four days of rest. But Zverev has looked calmer under pressure since Sinner's exit, his serve has been rock-solid, and his clay-court pedigree should prevail in the longer rallies that define Roland Garros finals. Zverev in four sets, finally claiming his first major.
