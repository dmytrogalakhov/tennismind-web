Despite being called 'clay courts,' Roland Garros surfaces are actually made of layers of sand and volcanic rocks topped with white limestone and red brick dust. The red brick dust creates the distinctive color and playing characteristics that make the French Open unique among Grand Slams. This complex composition explains why the surface plays so differently from other courts — the ball bounces higher and slower, demanding different tactical approaches from players.
