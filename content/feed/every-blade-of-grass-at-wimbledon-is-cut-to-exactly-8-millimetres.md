All 38 courts at the All England Club — 18 match courts including Centre Court and No. 1 Court, plus 20 practice courts — are trimmed to precisely 8 millimetres before the Championships begin. That uniformity creates the vivid, consistent shade of green that has become one of Wimbledon's most recognisable visual signatures. It also means the bounce and pace players experience on a practice court should, in theory, be identical to what they face on Centre Court.
