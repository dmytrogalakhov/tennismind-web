Jannik Sinner beat Casper Ruud 6-4, 6-4 to win his first Rome title and complete the Career Golden Masters — all nine ATP Masters 1000 trophies — becoming only the second man in history after Novak Djokovic to achieve it. He did it at 24, seven years younger than Djokovic, and became the first Italian to win the Rome title since Adriano Panatta in 1976.
