MEN'S DRAW
Djokovic refused to yield in five hours of grass-court theatre, Sinner kept his powder dry, and the semifinal picture is now set.

• Djokovic🇷🇸 def. Auger-Aliassime🇨🇦 in five — five hours and fifteen minutes, two tiebreaks, and a fifth-set nerve war settled 7-6: at 39, Djokovic becomes the oldest major semifinalist since Ken Rosewall in 1977 and extends his record to eight consecutive Wimbledon semifinal appearances; he said he still plays for moments like this, and the draw is now his to lose
• Sinner🇮🇹 def. Struff🇩🇪 — the defending champion saved a set point in the second set but was off court in two and a half hours, four straight-sets wins into the tournament and his physical management immaculate; the top half of the draw is consolidating around a Djokovic collision
• Zverev🇩🇪 def. Lehecka🇨🇿 in four — a match suspended overnight for curfew, Lehecka winning three straight games to open Tuesday before Zverev steadied and closed out the fourth-set tiebreak; his first Wimbledon quarterfinal, and the bottom half of the draw now belongs to him or Djokovic

WOMEN'S DRAW
The semifinal lineup is set, and it could not be more compelling.

• Gauff🇺🇸 def. Pegula🇺🇸 in three — dropped the opener before winning twelve of the last sixteen games against a fellow American; with Sabalenka and Krejcikova already gone, Gauff is now among the draw's genuine title favourites
• Muchova🇨🇿 def. Osaka🇯🇵 — having ended Osaka's deepest-ever Wimbledon run in straight sets, Muchova is widely considered the most natural grass-court player left in the draw; her volleys, slices, and exquisite shotmaking set up a semifinal against Gauff that is the match of the tournament
