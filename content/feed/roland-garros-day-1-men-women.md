MEN'S DRAW
No. 2 Alexander Zverev cruised past Benjamin Bonzi 6-3, 6-4, 6-2, while Novak Djokovic began his 25th Grand Slam quest with a four-set victory over Giovanni Mpetshi Perricard in the night session. The day's biggest upset saw No. 7 Taylor Fritz fall to American Nishesh Basavareddy in four sets. No. 28 seed Joao Fonseca defeated French qualifier Luka Pavlovic, while James Duckworth advanced when Gabriel Diallo retired at 6-3, 4-1.

WOMEN'S DRAW
No. 8 Mirra Andreeva overcame French wild card Fiona Ferro in straight sets, while No. 15 Marta Kostyuk swept past Oksana Selekhmeteva 6-2, 6-3. In the day's marathon match, No. 26 Hailey Baptiste outlasted 2021 champion Barbora Krejcikova in a near three-hour, three-set thriller to advance to the second round.

STAT OF THE DAY
This marks the 125th edition of the French Open and the 96th Grand Slam tournament, making it the oldest of the four majors still contested at its original venue.
