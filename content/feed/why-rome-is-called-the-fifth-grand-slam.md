The 2026 Italian Open offers a combined $17.9 million in prize money — $9.6 million for men and $8.3 million for women. That's more than most Masters 1000 events and closing in on Grand Slam territory. Combined with its best-of-five format in later rounds, its history dating back to 1930, and its role as the final clay-court dress rehearsal before Roland Garros, Rome's claim to being the "fifth Slam" gets stronger every year.
