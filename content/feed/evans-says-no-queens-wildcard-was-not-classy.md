Dan Evans, who is set to retire after this season, publicly called out the Queen's Club organisers for not offering him a wildcard into what would have been one of his final tournaments on home soil, saying it would have been 'a classy gesture.' Evans has been a fixture at Queen's throughout his career and the snub drew attention given the tournament's history of honouring British players. With Wimbledon his likely farewell Slam, the absence of a Queen's send-off leaves a gap in what could have been a fitting domestic goodbye.
