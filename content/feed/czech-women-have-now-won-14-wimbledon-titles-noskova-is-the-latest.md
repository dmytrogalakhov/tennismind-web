Linda Noskova's victory over Karolina Muchova on Saturday completed an all-Czech Wimbledon women's final — and extended one of tennis's most remarkable national records. Czech women have now won the Wimbledon singles title 14 times: Martina Navratilova claimed nine between 1978 and 1990, Jana Novotna won in 1998, Petra Kvitova in 2011 and 2014, Barbora Krejcikova in 2024, and now Noskova in 2026. No other country comes close to that haul on the grass of the All England Club. What makes it stranger is that the Czech Republic is a country of 10 million people — smaller than many European capitals. Something about how Czech tennis develops players, particularly on the women's side, keeps producing Wimbledon champions.
