Sabalenka's serving dominance and Grand Slam experience clash with Shnaider's aggressive baseline power in their first career meeting. Shnaider can trouble the world No. 1 if she converts break chances early and maintains the fearless aggression that carried her past Madison Keys in three sets. But Sabalenka hasn't dropped a set all tournament and brings the kind of big-match composure that matters most in first-time major semifinalists, especially after her commanding straight-sets wins over Kasatkina and Osaka. Sabalenka in straights to reach her second consecutive Roland Garros final.
