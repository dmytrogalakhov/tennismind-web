Osaka's power game and Grand Slam pedigree collide with a Jacquemot who is badly out of form but playing on a surface where neither woman has a commanding record. Jacquemot's upset path runs entirely through Osaka's leg injury — if the retirement in Bad Homburg has left Osaka moving cautiously, the Frenchwoman's flat ball-striking and willingness to trade groundstrokes could stretch rallies and expose that vulnerability. The problem is Jacquemot has won just two of her last twelve matches, and Osaka arrived at Wimbledon having just played a grass-court final, meaning her timing and confidence on the surface are in better shape than her fitness alone would suggest. Osaka in straight sets, but watch the first few games for any sign she's protecting that leg.
