MEN'S DRAW
Day 3 delivered major upsets as No. 6 Daniil Medvedev crashed out to Australian Adam Walton in five sets, opening up Jannik Sinner's draw significantly. French teenager Moïse Kouamé provided the day's emotional highlight, becoming the youngest man to win a Grand Slam match in 17 years by defeating 2014 US Open champion Marin Čilić in straight sets. The 17-year-old from Paris received a thunderous ovation from the home crowd after his breakthrough victory. No. 4 Félix Auger-Aliassime survived a major scare against Daniel Altmaier, winning a grueling five-setter that ended in a final-set tiebreak.

WOMEN'S DRAW
The biggest shock saw No. 5 Jessica Pegula fall to Kimberly Birrell, who rallied from a set down to stun the American. No. 2 Aryna Sabalenka advanced comfortably to set up a second-round clash with French player Elsa Jacquemot. Rising American teenager Iva Jović continued her impressive run, defeating Alexandra Eala in straight sets on Court 14.
