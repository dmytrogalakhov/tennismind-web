WOMEN'S DRAW

Mirra Andreeva demolished Marta Kostyuk 6-1, 6-3 to reach her first Grand Slam final, becoming the youngest woman to reach the Roland Garros final since Monica Seles in 1991. The 17-year-old Russian reversed their recent head-to-head deficit with a dominant performance, briefly wobbling when she missed a chance for a 5-1 lead but quickly regaining control when Kostyuk double-faulted to open her next service game. Polish qualifier Maja Chwalińska completed one of the most extraordinary Grand Slam runs in recent memory, defeating Diana Shnaider 7-6, 6-4 to become only the fourth Polish woman to reach a major final. The world No. 114, playing her first Roland Garros main draw, won a marathon 77-minute opening set before closing out the match in two hours and 13 minutes, setting up an unlikely final between two of the tournament's biggest breakthrough stories.
